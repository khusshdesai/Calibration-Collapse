An empirical machine learning research project exploring how LLM-generated synthetic training data affects the probabilistic calibration of downstream transformer classifiers, and testing a heuristic mitigation strategy.

---

## 📖 Overview
**What problem the project solves:** 
When models are trained on synthetic data generated by LLMs, they successfully learn to classify text but often become systematically overconfident. Their predicted probabilities no longer reflect actual accuracy—a phenomenon we explore as **Calibration Collapse**.

**Why it exists:** 
To investigate how traditional calibration techniques (like Temperature Scaling) perform under fully synthetic data conditions, and to evaluate a heuristic method (SATS) designed to calibrate mixed-data models without requiring a human-labeled validation set.

**Who it is intended for:** 
AI researchers and developers working with synthetic datasets who need reliable confidence scores from their classification models.

**Key value:** 
Highlights the hidden risks of replacing human data entirely with LLM-generated data, and offers a practical scaling technique to restore confidence reliability in mixed training regimes.

## ✨ Features
### 1. Calibration Evaluation Pipeline
- **What it does:** Measures the Expected Calibration Error (ECE) of models trained on varying ratios of synthetic-to-real data.
- **How it works:** Trains DistilBERT and RoBERTa classifiers across 5 discrete synthetic ratios (0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%) and evaluates their logit distributions.
- **Relevant files:** `src/experiment.py`, `src/experiment_roberta.py`

### 2. Synthetic-Aware Temperature Scaling (SATS)
- **What it does:** A heuristic approach to calculate an optimal Temperature Scaling parameter ($T$) without needing a labeled validation set.
- **How it works:** Uses a simple linear regression ($T = \alpha \times X + \beta$) to map the mean magnitude of unscaled test logits to the required temperature scale, effectively reversing the logit inflation caused by synthetic data.
- **Relevant files:** `src/sats.py`, `src/run_sats_analysis.py`

### 3. Data Generation Scripts
- **What it does:** Generates the synthetic datasets used in the experiments.
- **How it works:** Uses LLaMA 3.2 (for AG News topic classification) and Gemma 2 (for SST-2 sentiment analysis) to synthesize training samples.
- **Relevant files:** `src/generate_data.py`, `src/generate_sst2.py`

## 📊 The Phenomenon: Calibration Collapse
As the proportion of synthetic data increases, models learn to inflate logit magnitudes due to the reduced linguistic diversity in LLM-generated text. This leads to overconfident predictions.

While Temperature Scaling improves calibration in mixed regimes (achieving a 1.80% ECE at a 75% synthetic mix), a residual 8.42% error persists at the 100% extreme. This suggests that purely synthetic datasets introduce structural logit imbalances that simple scaling cannot fully fix.*

## 🚀 Synthetic-Aware Temperature Scaling (SATS)
We test SATS as a way to achieve validation-free calibration. By modeling the logit inflation caused by synthetic data, SATS predicts the optimal Temperature Scaling parameter directly from unscaled test-time logit statistics.

## 🏗 Architecture & Data Flow
The architecture is designed around a reproducible, sequential ML pipeline:

1. **Generation:** `generate_data.py` interfaces with LLMs to output synthetic `.csv` files.
2. **Training:** `experiment_multiseed.py` runs training loops across multiple random seeds, fine-tuning HuggingFace models on dynamic mixes of real/synthetic data.
3. **Inference Extraction:** Raw logits (before softmax) are saved as `.npy` arrays to decouple neural network inference from calibration analysis.
4. **Calibration:** `sats.py` ingests the `.npy` logits, computes statistics, fits the SATS regression, and outputs scaled metrics.

## 🚧 Engineering Decisions
**Decoupling Inference from Calibration:** 
Instead of calculating ECE directly during the PyTorch training loop, we chose to save raw logits as `.npy` arrays. While this consumes more disk space, it allows the `SATSCalibrator` to run rapid, iterative experiments on CPU without requiring the GPU to re-run inference for every test.

## 🌟 Project Highlights
- **Modular Pipeline:** Separated heavy tensor inference from fast heuristic calibration analysis to speed up experimentation.
- **Validation-Free Calibration:** Evaluated a heuristic (SATS) that predicts Temperature Scaling parameters based on logit variance ($\Delta T < 0.06$ error in our tests).
- **Empirical Rigor:** Ran multi-seed training loops across 5 data ratios, 2 architectures, and 2 dataset domains to verify the collapse phenomenon.
